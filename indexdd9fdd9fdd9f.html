What is Miner Hosting?

Miner hosting is an infrastructure-level digital asset configuration model where users purchase mining machines, and professional mining facilities manage, operate, and maintain them on their behalf, with profits belonging to the user.

In the miner hosting model, investors retain ownership of the equipment, while professional mining farms handle deployment, power supply, networking, cooling, maintenance, and security. Once operational, the equipment participates in cryptocurrency network mining (e.g., Bitcoin) and generates continuous output based on its hash power contribution. Earnings (BTC or equivalent) are distributed to the user's account daily in proportion to their hash power.

This model combines asset ownership with professional operational capabilities, suitable for long-term investors, institutional funds, or individuals seeking transparent investment structures.

How Miner Hosting Works

Equipment Procurement

Users purchase ASIC mining machines (e.g., Bitmain Antminer series) that meet the mining farm's standards through the platform or certified third-party channels.

Warehouse Registration

Upon delivery to the mining facility, the platform completes equipment numbering, ownership registration, and hardware inspection, binding the equipment to the user's exclusive account.

Deployment & Configuration

Our engineering team connects the equipment to the power grid, network nodes, and cooling system, completing mining pool setup and system configuration for optimal performance.

24/7 Operation & Maintenance

The system monitors equipment temperature, voltage, current, and hashrate in real-time, with automatic alerts for anomalies and prompt handling by our engineering team.

Revenue Settlement

Daily earnings are allocated to the user's account based on hash power contribution, typically in BTC, with support for conversion to stablecoins like USDT.

Real-time Monitoring

Users can log in to the dashboard at any time to view transparent data including equipment status, power consumption, cumulative earnings, and hashrate trends.

Eligibility for Miner Hosting

ASIC Miner Ownership

Users must own ASIC equipment meeting our standards for model, energy efficiency, interface, and power supply (supporting platform procurement or user-provided devices).

Service Agreement

Users sign a formal "Miner Hosting Service Agreement" clarifying equipment ownership, platform responsibilities, pricing mechanisms, and exit procedures.

Power & Hosting Fees

Users are billed periodically (usually monthly) for power consumption and hosting services, with some plans having minimum volume or prepayment requirements.

Long-Term Investment

Miner hosting is ideal for investment horizons of 12-36 months to account for equipment depreciation and ensure expected returns.

Benefits of Miner Hosting

Passive Income Stream

Daily BTC earnings without needing to trade or monitor market fluctuations, providing stable, traceable income.

Professional O&M

Avoid technical complexities; our experts handle wiring, power management, networking, cooling, and mining pool operations.

No Home Disruptions

Avoid noise, heat, and high electricity bills from home mining setups, maintaining a normal living or working environment.

Core Value of Miner Hosting

  • Clear Asset Ownership

    Users retain full ownership of the equipment, with earnings based on actual hash power performance, not platform promises or contracts.

  • Hardware-Driven Revenue

    Earnings derive from real equipment operation and network participation, not speculative models or platform tokens.

  • Full Transparency

    Access to equipment operation logs, power consumption data, and earnings reports for audit-friendly digital asset management.

  • Predictable ROI

    Structured model based on equipment cost + ongoing power expenses × hash rate, with clear ROI projections and exit strategies.

  • Blockchain Ecosystem Participation

    Equipment contributes to blockchain network security and transaction validation, making investors part of global blockchain infrastructure.

Who Should Consider Miner Hosting?

Individual & Institutional Investors

Investors prioritizing "visible output and transparent structures" for their digital asset portfolios.

Family Wealth Planners

High-net-worth individuals and families seeking to build the BTC asset base as part of long-term wealth planning.

Traditional Asset Investors

Investors unfamiliar with technical operations but wanting to participate in the blockchain hash power economy.

Corporate & Fund Investors

Enterprises and institutions diversifying assets to avoid risks associated with platform tokens or contract products.
